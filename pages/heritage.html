Grandparents Generation

Grandfather: Mr. Odarmetey (Laryea)

Birth Name: Laryea

Known As: Mr. Odarmetey

The Story Behind the Name: As a child, Mr. Laryea was under the care of Mr. Odarmetey. When he went to school, there was a mistake with his name registration, and he was enrolled as "Odarmetey." Rather than correct this error when he grew up, he chose to keep the name Odarmetey as a way to honor the man who had cared for him. However, all of his children carry the family name Laryea, preserving the original family lineage.

Legacy: A testament to gratitude and honor - choosing to carry the name of someone who showed him care and kindness.

Family Traditions & Values

Core Values Passed Down:

  • Honor and Gratitude: Exemplified by Mr. Laryea's choice to keep the Odarmetey name to honor his caregiver
  • Family Unity: Staying connected and supporting each other across generations
  • Respect for Elders: Honoring those who came before us and their sacrifices
  • Cultural Pride: Remembering and celebrating our heritage and roots
  • Legacy Preservation: Maintaining family names and stories for future generations
  • Education as Foundation: Mr. Odarmetey established that every family member is required to achieve at least a degree or some level of recognized educational qualification. This requirement stems from his own missed educational opportunities - he enforced this rule to ensure that future generations would have the chances he never had, breaking the cycle and creating a foundation for success.

Family Traditions:

  • Sharing family stories to preserve our history
  • Celebrating milestones and achievements together
  • Honoring the memory of our ancestors
  • Maintaining connections across different family branches
  • First Paycheck Blessing Tradition: When someone receives their first paycheck, they bring it to the nuclear family. Each family member can take any amount from the paycheck (or simply touch it as a symbolic gesture) and then pray for the person or bless the money. After everyone has participated, the whole family joins together in prayer for the person's success and prosperity. This beautiful tradition typically sees family members taking modest amounts (the highest known being around $5), ensuring it doesn't burden the earner while creating a meaningful blessing ceremony that celebrates new beginnings and family support.

Special Family Stories:

  • The story of Mr. Laryea's name change - a testament to gratitude and honor
  • Rhodalyn's recent name change from Nerissa - continuing the tradition of meaningful names
  • The preservation of both Laryea and Nortey family names across generations
  • Mr. Odarmetey's Educational Legacy - Despite not having the educational opportunities himself, he established a family requirement that every member must achieve at least a degree or recognized educational qualification, ensuring his descendants would have the chances he was denied
